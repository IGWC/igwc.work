Indiana Graduate Workers Coalition Celebrates Five Years of Improving Graduate Student Life

Today the IGWC celebrates its five-year anniversary.

Although the union now represents a majority of Student Academic Appointees, the organization began with a town hall on September 12, 2019, which aimed to end mandatory student fees. From two hundred grad workers who attended the town hall to 2,000 who signed a petition to end all student fees, the union quickly gained momentum in its effort to improve the lives of its members. 

Since then, we have accomplished enormous victories together. Organizing has led to: 

  • The end of $1,435 in mandatory student fees
  • The waiver of  the international student fee and the G901 fee
  • A 53% increase in minimum SAA stipends (from $15,000 to the current $23,000 per year for 10-month contracts)
  • Improvements to healthcare and other benefits
  • More representation at the Bloomington Faculty Council

The union expresses its deepest gratitude to its members and supporters, without whom its victories would have been impossible. 

Much work still lies ahead of us. We are still fighting for a living wage, which is now $34,534 for 10-month contracts and $41,441 for 12-month contracts, and union recognition. More recently, the administration’s new free expression policy now threatens our ability to assemble as workers and the state government’s bill SEA 202 censors graduate worker instruction. 

More than ever, we must work together to rebuild IU. 

If you know any new graduate students, we ask you to please introduce them to our organization. 

To all supporters of our union, we kindly ask for donations in our fight for the freedom of speech and assembly.

To all current union members, please consider paying anywhere from $5 to $32 per month in voluntary union dues.
