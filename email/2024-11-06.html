The Path Forwards

Things feel dire. The austerity, overreach, and repression from IU administration is deeply entangled with the larger national currents. Questions like “What do we do?” and “What can we do?” seem both overwhelming and important.

What do we do?

Organizing starts small: talk to your peers inside and outside your department, or join in a work session on Mondays 6pm at Canterbury House (719 E. 7th St) or Thursdays 6pm at Atlas. Build connections and community wherever you can.

These connections and community help build resilience, but a key component of organizing is realizing you are a part of a larger collective that takes action: showing up to rallies or other demonstrations is one good way to show solidarity with each other.

There are several rallies in the few weeks where you can show that solidarity:

Friday, November 8, 7pm at the Courthouse Square:
A rally organized by local Bloomington highschoolers to protest IU administration’s curtailing of free expression, especially around issues of Palestinian solidarity.

Friday, December 6, Noon near Sample Gates:
Whitten Goes/Union Grows Rally hosted by the IGWC. The rally is multi-sector (graduate workers, undergraduates, faculty, staff are all welcome), centered around the end of Pamela Whitten’s presidency, the repeal of the repressive free expression policy, and union recognition. We want a better IU, and these demands are the small first steps towards building it. We need to show each other that we’re fighting for something bigger. RSVP at igwc.work/rally.

There are other ways to organize too. If you’re a graduate student, sign a union card, talk to your friends about the IGWC, and join the open organizing meetings on Mondays at 4pm at Canterbury House (719 E. 7th St)—those are all good ways to get plugged in.

If you’re a faculty member, coordinate within and across departments to build up connections and solidarity between students, faculty, and staff.

If you’re a staff member, consider joining the CWA Local 4818 at IU, and building solidarity and safety through numbers.

There’s still work to be done.

Last Friday, a small group of graduate workers from across IU submitted a letter to the Board of Trustees demanding the termination of Whitten’s presidency, the suspension of the restrictions on free speech through UA-10, and union recognition. In the letter, the IGWC outlines the reasoning behind each demand. The Board of Trustees must respond by November 20th, or the IGWC “will move forward with [its] own objectives for ensuring the university remains a world-class educational and research institution.”

First in that campaign is a multi-coalition rally of students, faculty, staff and others in the IU community held on Friday, December 6 at noon. RSVP and show solidarity at igwc.work/rally.
