Board of Trustees Ignores IGWC Demand Letter: Rally December 6 at Noon near Sample Gates

It's a busy time of year! Between grading, research, and the holidays, it can sometimes be hard to keep up with everything. We can only assume this is also a busy time for the IU Board of Trustees too, since they refused to respond to our request for a meeting. Although they sent an email acknowledging receipt of our letter, they didn't bother drafting any response. They must have been busy raising the cost housing and dining for undergraduates, dodging demands to disclose the university's investment in the military industrial complex made by pro-Palestinian organizations, approving devastating funding cuts to programs across the university, or setting aside hundreds of thousands of dollars in bonuses for Pamela Whitten and other top administrators despite ubiquitous calls for the termination of her presidency

It’s now time for the entire IU community to gather together and let our voices be heard loud and clear.

On Friday at Noon, December 6, near Sample Gates, we'll rally to demand the termination of Whitten's presidency, the repeal of the free expression policy, and union recognition.

We invite all faculty, staff, students, and community members to join the IGWC and demand that the Board of Trustees recognize the will of the IU community and restore shared governance.
